Group therapy is a type of psychotherapy where a group of individuals facing similar psychological challenges come together to work under the guidance of professionals in a safe therapeutic environment. Group therapy allows participants to connect with others going through similar experiences in a safe setting, fostering a sense of security, mutual support, and understanding.

  • Group support: Participants can receive support from others who are going through similar experiences.
  • Learning from others: You will learn from the experiences of others and gain new strategies for dealing with your challenges.
  • Increased self-awareness: You can gain a deeper understanding of yourself through the observations of others.
  • A safe environment: A safe environment is provided that allows you to express yourself freely and without judgment.
  • Lower cost: Group therapy is often less expensive than individual therapy.

Group psychotherapy is for individuals experiencing the same psychological distress or symptoms, such as anxiety, loss, burnout, and other challenges. Group therapy is suitable for those seeking to improve their mental health or achieve psychological balance through group interaction and support. Participants are selected based on how well their needs align with the session's objectives.

The maximum number of participants is typically 14. This allows ample time for each individual to interact and participate.

Individual therapy focuses on direct interaction between the client and therapist to address personal issues. Group therapy, on the other hand, focuses on interaction among a group of individuals facing similar challenges. This reduces feelings of isolation and promotes healing through shared experiences, mutual support, and learning from others.
